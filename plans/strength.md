# Strength Program — Ironman 70.3 Support

Companion to `macro.md` (*Strength Periodization* section). Turns "Gym ×2" into an actual
program that periodizes with the training year and doesn't sabotage key swim/bike/run
sessions.

**Goal of lifting here**: injury resistance, durability, and economy — *not* muscle mass.
Strength supports the race; it never competes with it. When strength and a key endurance
session conflict, the endurance session wins.

---

## The sequencing fix (why Mon/Wed are split this way)

Key quality endurance sessions land on **Tue (run)**, **Thu (bike)**, and the **Sat/Sun**
long block. Heavy legs the day before any of those degrades them. Given the two gym days
are pinned to **Mon** and **Wed**, the split is:

| Day | Focus | Why |
|-----|-------|-----|
| **Mon** | **Lower body + posterior chain** (the primary strength day) | Precedes Tue's run, which is now *tempo* (not max VO2), so mild residual fatigue is tolerable. Heavy/low-rep work causes far less next-day soreness than high-rep hypertrophy. |
| **Wed** | **Upper body + core + hip/ankle stability/mobility** (low leg load) | Keeps legs fresh for Thu's quality bike. |

On **deload weeks**, gym drops to **1× light** (or skip the second session) — the whole
week deloads, strength included.

## Core movement menu (rotate within these)

- **Squat pattern**: goblet squat → back/front squat
- **Hinge**: Romanian deadlift, hip thrust, single-leg RDL
- **Single-leg** (highest value for tri): Bulgarian split squat, step-ups, walking lunges
- **Calf/foot**: standing + seated calf raises, single-leg hops (run durability)
- **Push**: push-ups, DB bench/overhead press
- **Pull**: rows, lat pulldown/pull-ups (swim-relevant)
- **Core/anti-rotation**: plank variations, dead bug, Pallof press, side plank, bird-dog
- **Power** (Build/Peak only): box jumps, broad jumps, med-ball throws

## Periodization

### Base (now → ~2026-09) — Anatomical Adaptation → Max Strength
Build tissue tolerance and clean technique, then start getting genuinely strong.
- **Weeks 1–4**: adaptation — 2–3 × 12–15, moderate load, full-body both days. Nail form.
- **Weeks 5+**: strength — main lifts **4 × 5–6** heavy, accessories 2–3 × 8–12.
- Progress load when all reps are clean. This is the window to lift heaviest — endurance
  intensity is still low, so recovery budget exists.

### Build (~2026-10 → 2027-02) — Max Strength + Power
Maintain strength on less volume; introduce explosive work.
- Main lifts **3–5 × 3–5** heavy (fewer sets, keep intensity).
- Add **power**: 3–4 × 3–5 box jumps / med-ball throws (quality, fully rested reps).
- Cut accessory volume as bike/run intensity climbs. Two short focused sessions > two long.

### Peak (2027-03 → ~2027-05-02) — Maintenance / Power
Protect the legs for bricks and race-pace work.
- **1–2 × week**, ~30–40 min: 1–2 heavy sets of a main lift + a little power + core.
- Enough to *retain* strength (you can't build meaningfully here anyway); no soreness that
  bleeds into key sessions. **Drop to 1×/week in the final 4 weeks.**

### Taper (2027-05-03 → race)
- One light full-body session early in the taper for feel; **stop lifting ~10 days out.**
- No new stimulus — only maintenance of feel and mobility.

## Sample sessions

**Monday (Base, strength block) — Lower ~50–60 min**
1. Warm-up: hips/ankles mobility, bodyweight squats, glute activation (8 min)
2. Back/Goblet squat — 4 × 5
3. Romanian deadlift — 3 × 6
4. Bulgarian split squat — 3 × 8 / leg
5. Standing calf raise — 3 × 12
6. Pallof press — 3 × 10 / side

**Wednesday (Base) — Upper + Core ~45–55 min**
1. Warm-up: band shoulder work, T-spine mobility (6 min)
2. DB bench or push-up progression — 3 × 8–10
3. Row (barbell/DB) — 4 × 8
4. Overhead press — 3 × 8
5. Pull-ups / lat pulldown — 3 × max-2
6. Core circuit ×3: plank 45s · dead bug 10/side · side plank 30s/side

**Monday (Peak) — Full-body maintenance ~35 min**
1. Squat — 2 × 4 (heavy-ish, leave reps in reserve)
2. Box jumps — 3 × 3
3. Single-leg RDL — 2 × 6 / leg
4. Core: Pallof + plank, 2 rounds

---

## Rules of thumb

- **Never** grind a lift to failure the day before a key endurance session.
- Leave **1–2 reps in reserve** most of the time — this is support work, not powerlifting.
- If a session feels awful, cut it to the main lift + core and leave. Recovery > volume.
- Fuel protein through the day (~1.6 g/kg ≈ ~110 g for 69 kg); lifting on top of endurance
  raises the requirement.
- Log what you actually did (see the training log) — "Gym 60min" tells future-you nothing.
